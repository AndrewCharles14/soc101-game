SOC101: Life Through Society

A scenario-based game about social structures, identity, and inequality

📋 Sample Turn

Scenario: It's picture day. Your teacher says to wear something "appropriate for your gender." You don't like the suggested options.

Choice A, go along with it: Belonging +5, Opportunity -5 (you feel unseen).

Choice B, wear what you want: Opportunity +5, Belonging -5 (social cost for self-expression).

Sociology Lesson: This illustrates gender socialization, which is how we learn society's expectations about dress and behavior based on perceived gender. Schools are one of the key agents of socialization.

Concepts tagged: Gender Socialization · Social Norms · Agents of Socialization

📚 Assignment Info & Purpose

Purpose: Apply SOC101 concepts like health inequality, gender socialization, and structural inequality to realistic life scenarios.

Players: 1 player at a time. Easy to pass around for group play.

How to Start: Click "Start Game," read instructions, choose a social profile, and play 5 rounds.

How a Turn Works: Read a short scenario → choose from 2–4 options → see the consequence + a sociology explanation → scores update.

How Points Work: Decisions adjust 5 categories: Opportunity, Health, Stability, Belonging, and Resources. There is no single winning score. The goal is to notice how social forces shape outcomes.

How the Game Ends: After Round 5 you see a results summary and four reflection questions for class discussion.

How to Play

🎯 Goal

Navigate 5 life scenarios shaped by social structures, identity, and institutions. Your choices affect 5 score categories. At the end, reflect on how society, not just personal choices, shaped your outcome.

📊 Score Categories

  • 🎓 Opportunity: Access to education, advancement, and choices
  • ❤️ Health: Wellbeing and healthcare access
  • 🏠 Stability: Economic security and safety nets
  • 🤝 Belonging: Community, identity affirmation, social support
  • 💰 Resources: Income, assets, and material access

  2. Round 2: School, Peers & Identity
  3. Round 3: Work, Insurance & Inequality

📖 Key Concept Glossary

Gender Socialization: The process by which family, school, peers, and media teach us gender roles and expectations.

Healthcare as a Social Institution: Access to care is shaped by income, insurance type, race, and geography, not just individual choices.

Heteronormativity / Cisnormativity: Social assumptions that everyone is heterosexual and cisgender, which creates barriers for LGBTQ+ individuals.

Conflict Theory: Capitalism and class inequality shape who gets what, including healthcare, education, and opportunity. Health becomes commodified.

Functionalism & the Sick Role (Talcott Parsons): Sickness is a socially sanctioned form of deviance with rights (rest, exemption from duties) and responsibilities (seek care, try to recover). The physician acts as gatekeeper.

Agents of Socialization: Family, school, peer groups, and mass media all transmit social norms and expectations.

Sex vs. Gender: Sex is biological and physiological. Gender is socially constructed and culturally variable. They are not interchangeable.

ACA / Obamacare: The Affordable Care Act expanded insurance coverage to millions of previously uninsured Americans through marketplaces and Medicaid expansion.

Socialized vs. Universal Healthcare: The UK's NHS is socialized, which means it is government owned and operated. Canada's system is universal, which means it is publicly funded but privately delivered. The U.S. is largely private but already has public programs like Medicare, Medicaid, CHIP, the VA, and the Indian Health Service.
